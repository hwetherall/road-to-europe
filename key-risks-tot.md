Key Risks
Risk 1
high threat
Chelsea sit sixth on 48 points with a goal difference of +15 — comfortably the best goal difference of any side between 5th and 10th — which speaks to a team that doesn't just win games but wins them convincingly. Chelsea are firmly in the European qualification race and will treat this home fixture as a chance to consolidate, meaning there will be no let-up in intensity. For Tottenham, mired in a relegation battle in 17th place with just seven wins all season, travelling to a well-resourced Chelsea side at Stamford Bridge represents one of the most hostile environments on the calendar — the gulf in squad confidence and league standing could not be starker.
Risk 2
medium threat
The simulation data tells you everything about the asymmetry of this fixture: a Chelsea win swings Tottenham's relegation probability by a devastating -18.3 percentage points, the single largest negative swing of any remaining result. A defeat here doesn't just cost Tottenham three points — it likely emboldens West Ham (currently one point behind) and creates a psychological collapse scenario where the remaining fixtures feel increasingly impossible. The cascading damage of a loss at Stamford Bridge extends far beyond the match itself.
Risk 3
medium threat
Chelsea's home advantage is backed by the underlying numbers — they have accumulated 48 points from 31 games with 13 wins, a record that suggests they are winning roughly half their home-and-away matches, and with a +15 goal difference they are capable of punishing opponents who leave space. Tottenham's away record this season has contributed to their precarious position, and travelling to a top-six side represents the kind of fixture where the gap in squad quality and confidence can produce a lopsided result.
