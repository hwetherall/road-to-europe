Key Risks
Risk 1
high threat
Chelsea under Liam Rosenior have become more dangerous with quicker, less fussy attacking rotations, and the return of Cole Palmer and Nicolas Jackson was immediately cited as a major lift before and around this fixture. If Tottenham's wing-backs are caught high in Igor Tudor's back-three structure, Chelsea can attack the outside channels fast.
Risk 2
medium threat
Palmer is still Chelsea's clearest chance-creation hub and their game model increasingly runs through him finding pockets between the lines. Tottenham's recent structural issue under Tudor has been protecting wide areas and half-spaces at the same time, which is exactly where Palmer hurts teams.
Risk 3
medium threat
Chelsea's home control is the other danger: Rosenior was already being described by the Premier League as a pragmatist, and Spurs came into this period without a league win in 2026. If Chelsea score first, they have the profile to turn the match into territory and rest-defence rather than chaos.
Where the Matchup Favours Tottenham
1
Hit the space behind full-backs
Rosenior's Chelsea have leaned into wider rotations and more aggressive attacking patterns, which can leave space if the first press is beaten. Tudor's Spurs are built for vertical releases and wing-back running, so the best route is not slow possession but fast access into the channels Chelsea vacate.
2
Make chaos, not control
The Premier League's own analysis of Tudor points to a more vertical, pressing Spurs with wing-backs and aggressive movements from the outside centre-backs. Chelsea are more comfortable when they can settle the game; Tottenham's chance is to keep it stretched, second-ball heavy and transition-based.
3
Attack set-play moments hard
With Spurs badly hit by injuries, dead-ball situations become one of the cleanest ways to level the talent gap. Chelsea have attacking set-piece threats of their own, but Tottenham's survival path probably needs cheap goals, and corners/free-kicks are the quickest route to one.
What to Watch For
If Tottenham can break Chelsea's first line and release runners into the channels within the first 20 minutes, their best angle is alive.
Watch where Cole Palmer receives: if he is getting repeated touches between Tottenham's midfield and back line, Chelsea are controlling the match.
If Spurs are winning field position from throw-ins, corners and second balls rather than building slowly, the game is being played on the right terms for them.

It is the second-biggest direct swing game in the model, and one of the candidate survival paths is built around Spurs winning at Villa Park. 2. Tottenham Hotspur vs Everton
Why it matters
This is one of the more realistic pieces of the survival puzzle because it appears in the most plausible candidate path. Home games against teams in Tottenham's zone are where the model expects them to do the heavy lifting if one of the big away wins does not land.

3. Tottenham Hotspur vs Leeds United
Why it matters
This is the biggest positive swing among Tottenham's own remaining home fixtures in the sensitivity list, and it appears in two candidate paths. Beat Leeds and the whole equation changes because it is both a six-pointer and a direct cut into the relegation field around them.
Daniel Farke's Leeds are well-coached, but this is exactly the kind of home match Tottenham must convert if they are serious about staying up. The model gives this fixture enormous leverage, which usually means the table compression around both clubs is doing the work.

4. Crystal Palace vs West Ham United
Why it matters
This is one of the biggest non-Tottenham fixtures in the entire simulation set. If Palace beat Graham Potter's West Ham, one of the clubs around the lower-middle cluster drops points, and that gives Spurs more room for error elsewhere.
Oliver Glasner's Palace have a clear attacking reference point in Eberechi Eze and are capable of turning home matches into fast, direct contests. The model's sensitivity here is enormous, which tells you the table around Spurs is tightly packed enough for outside results to matter.

The Bottom Line
The cleanest route is simple: steal one major away win, then cash in at home against the teams around them. Chelsea away is the headline swing, but Leeds and Everton at home may be the fixtures that actually finish the job. Tottenham do not need perfection — they need precision.

The Scenario to Build Towards
The standout plausible route is: beat Chelsea away, lose at Aston Villa if necessary, then beat Everton at home — or, in the alternative path, turn Villa into the upset and back it up by beating Leeds.
